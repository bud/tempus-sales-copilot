# Tempus Genomic Testing Portfolio — Product Knowledge Base

## xT CDx — Comprehensive Tissue-Based DNA Sequencing

**Overview:** xT CDx is an FDA-approved 648-gene tissue-based next-generation sequencing (NGS) test for molecular profiling of all malignant solid tumors. It uses a tumor + normal matched approach, sequencing both the tumor specimen and a matched normal blood or saliva sample.

**Key Capabilities:**
- Detects single nucleotide variants (SNVs), multi-nucleotide variants (MNVs), insertions and deletions (INDELs) across 648 genes
- Microsatellite instability (MSI) status using genome-wide analysis of 239 microsatellite loci (more comprehensive than traditional 5-marker PCR)
- Tumor Mutational Burden (TMB)
- Copy number variations (CNVs)
- Rearrangements/fusions in 22 genes
- HLA Class I genotyping
- Potential germline findings (with patient consent)
- Companion diagnostic (CDx) claims for colorectal cancer (CRC) patients

**Turnaround Time:** Approximately 10–14 calendar days from specimen receipt.

**Clinical Advantage — Tumor-Normal Matching:** By sequencing a matched normal sample alongside the tumor, xT CDx filters out germline variants from somatic findings. This reduces false-positive calls compared to tumor-only approaches, improving accuracy of somatic variant detection. Studies show a significant reduction in somatic false-positive calls with tumor-normal matching.

**Auto-Conversion Feature:** If submitted tissue is insufficient for xT CDx testing, the sample automatically converts to xF liquid biopsy — no reorder required. This eliminates delays from tissue insufficiency.

**Ordering:** Available through Tempus Hub (online portal), paper requisition, or directly from the provider's EHR system.

## xR — Whole-Transcriptome RNA Sequencing

**Overview:** xR provides whole-transcriptome RNA sequencing for solid tumors and hematologic malignancies. It identifies clinically relevant fusions across 100+ targeted genes and altered splicing events.

**Key Capabilities:**
- Unbiased fusion detection across the full transcriptome (not limited to known fusion partners)
- Detects fusions that DNA-only panels miss — studies show additional clinically actionable fusions identified with RNA sequencing in cases where DNA sequencing alone missed them
- Altered splicing events including MET exon 14 skipping and EGFRvIII
- Gene expression analysis

**Testing Add-ons Available with xR:**
- Immunohistochemistry (IHC) including PD-L1
- FISH (fluorescence in situ hybridization)
- MGMT Promoter Methylation analysis (critical for glioblastoma treatment decisions)

**Clinical Advantage — Finding What DNA Misses:** In a retrospective analysis, more patients were identified with unique clinically actionable fusions through RNA sequencing that were not detected by DNA sequencing alone. This is particularly relevant for:
- Lung cancer (ALK, ROS1, RET, NTRK fusions)
- Cholangiocarcinoma (FGFR2 fusions)
- Sarcomas (various gene fusions)
- Gliomas (gene fusions and splicing events)

## xF / xF+ — Liquid Biopsy (Circulating Tumor DNA)

**Overview:** xF is a 105-gene liquid biopsy panel; xF+ is an expanded 523-gene panel. Both detect genomic alterations from circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) in peripheral blood.

**Key Capabilities (xF — 105 genes):**
- Single nucleotide variants (SNVs) and insertions/deletions (INDELs) in 105 genes
- Copy number gains (CNGs) in 6 genes
- Gene rearrangements in 6 genes
- MSI-H status

**Key Capabilities (xF+ — 523 genes):**
- All xF capabilities plus expanded to 523 genes
- Blood-based TMB (bTMB)
- Variants potentially associated with clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CH)

**Clinical Advantage — Complementary to Tissue Testing:**
- In a metastatic pan-cancer analysis, 9% of patients with actionable variants had unique actionable alterations found in liquid biopsy that were NOT detected in solid tumor testing alone
- Non-invasive: simple blood draw, no biopsy needed
- Faster specimen collection: particularly valuable when tissue is inaccessible or insufficient
- Detects resistance mutations that emerge during treatment (useful for monitoring)

**Dual Testing Strategy:** Tempus recommends complementary tissue (xT CDx) and liquid biopsy (xF/xF+) testing, as actionable variants may be detected in one modality but not the other.

## xG / xG+ — Hereditary Cancer Testing (Germline)

**Overview:** xG (CancerNext®) is a 40-gene guidelines-based germline panel. xG+ (CancerNext-Expanded®) is a 77-gene panel with broader coverage. Both are powered by Ambry Genetics.

**Key Capabilities:**
- xG: 40 genes associated with hereditary breast, ovarian, pancreatic, prostate, colorectal, endometrial, gastric, small bowel, urothelial, and renal cancers
- xG+: 77 genes covering all xG cancers plus additional hereditary cancer syndromes
- +RNAinsight® add-on available for enhanced variant classification

**Turnaround Time:** Approximately 14–21 calendar days from specimen receipt.

**Specimen Types:** Peripheral blood, saliva, or skin punch biopsy.

**Clinical Advantage:**
- Tailors cancer treatment approaches based on inherited risk
- Clarifies risk for additional cancers in the patient
- Enables identification of at-risk family members
- Cascade testing available for at-risk relatives at no additional cost (within 90 days, up to third-degree relatives)
- Free genetic counseling available through Genome Medical partnership

## xE — Whole Exome Sequencing

**Overview:** xE is a 19,000+ gene DNA sequencing panel providing the broadest genomic coverage in the Tempus portfolio.

**Key Capabilities:**
- Comprehensive coverage of 19,000+ genes
- TMB assessment
- Testing add-ons: IHC analysis including PD-L1 and MMR proteins

**Use Case:** Research-oriented or complex cases where standard panels may miss rare or novel alterations. Useful for patients who have exhausted standard-of-care options and are seeking clinical trial eligibility.

## xM — Minimal Residual Disease (MRD) Monitoring

**Overview:** xM offers both tumor-naive and tumor-informed assays to detect residual disease or early cancer recurrence, along with immunotherapy (IO) treatment response monitoring.

**Key Capabilities:**
- Tumor-naive MRD detection (no prior tumor sequencing required)
- Tumor-informed MRD tracking (personalized to the patient's known mutations)
- Serial monitoring to track treatment response over time
- Early detection of recurrence before clinical or radiographic evidence

**Clinical Advantage:** Enables oncologists to make earlier intervention decisions based on molecular evidence rather than waiting for imaging or clinical symptoms.

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. Foundation Medicine (FoundationOne CDx)
- **Tempus advantage:** Tumor-normal matched sequencing reduces false positives from germline contamination. FoundationOne CDx uses a tumor-only approach.
- **Tempus advantage:** Auto-reflex to liquid biopsy (xF) when tissue is insufficient — no manual reorder needed.
- **Tempus advantage:** Comprehensive portfolio under one provider (tissue, liquid, hereditary, MRD, RNA) vs. needing multiple vendors.
- **Tempus advantage:** MGMT methylation available as xR add-on (relevant for neuro-oncology).

### vs. Guardant Health (Guardant360)
- **Tempus advantage:** Guardant is liquid biopsy-focused. Tempus offers both tissue-based (xT CDx) and liquid biopsy (xF/xF+) for a complete profiling strategy.
- **Tempus advantage:** FDA-approved CDx claims for CRC with xT CDx.
- **Tempus advantage:** Hereditary testing (xG/xG+) through Ambry Genetics — Guardant does not offer germline testing.

### vs. Caris Life Sciences (Molecular Intelligence)
- **Tempus advantage:** Dedicated whole-transcriptome RNA sequencing (xR) as a standalone test for comprehensive fusion detection.
- **Tempus advantage:** Tumor-normal matching for cleaner somatic variant calls.
- **Tempus advantage:** MRD monitoring (xM) for post-treatment tracking.
